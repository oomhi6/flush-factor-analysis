## Why Legs and Feet Swell (And Why It's Hard to Fix)

Fluid doesn't pool in your ankles by accident. There are real physiological reasons it happens, and most remedies only treat the symptom.

The main culprits behind chronic lower-body swelling:

- **AVP hormone imbalance** — When AVP stays elevated, your body retains more fluid than it should, and it tends to collect downward due to gravity
- **Poor venous circulation** — Blood returning from your feet has to fight uphill. Anything that weakens that flow means fluid gets left behind
- **Sedentary time** — Sitting or standing for hours slows circulation; muscles that usually pump blood back up the leg aren't contracting
- **Dietary triggers** — High sodium, low potassium, low hydration all push the body toward retention
- **Age-related vascular changes** — Blood vessel walls lose elasticity over time, making efficient fluid movement harder

The frustrating part? Compression socks deal with #3 but nothing else. Elevation helps temporarily but doesn't change the underlying hormonal or circulatory picture. That's why a supplement targeting multiple pathways simultaneously — AVP balance, circulation support, and natural diuretic activity — has practical logic behind it.
